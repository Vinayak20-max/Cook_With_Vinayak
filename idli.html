RECIPE:



Ingredients:


  • 2 cups idli rice (or regular rice)
  • ½ cup urad dal (whole or split black lentils)
  • 1 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi seeds, optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (for soaking and grinding)
  • Oil (for greasing the idli plates)

    • Instructions:



    Soaking the Ingredients:

    Wash and Soak Rice:
    Rinse the idli rice thoroughly in water a few times until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in enough water for 4-6 hours.

    Soak Urad Dal and Fenugreek Seeds:
    Rinse the urad dal and fenugreek seeds (if using). Soak them together in enough water for 4-6 hours, separate from the rice.

    Grinding the Batter:
    Grind Urad Dal: Drain the water from the urad dal. Using a wet grinder or high-power blender, grind the urad dal into a smooth and fluffy batter. Add water gradually (about ½-1 cup), and ensure the batter is light and airy. It should have a thick yet flowing consistency.

    Grind the Rice:
    Drain the soaked rice. Grind it to a slightly coarse, grainy texture using a wet grinder or blender, adding water as needed. The rice batter should be thicker than the urad dal batter, but still spreadable.

    Combine the Batter:
    Mix the rice batter and urad dal batter together in a large bowl. Add salt and mix thoroughly using your hand (this helps in fermentation). The batter should be thick but not too watery.

    Fermenting the Batter:


    Fermentation:
    Cover the bowl and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours (or overnight) until it doubles in size and becomes bubbly. In cold weather, place the batter in an oven with the light on to aid fermentation.

    Steaming the Idlis:

    Prepare the Idli Steamer:
    Grease the idli plates with a little oil to prevent sticking. Pour the fermented batter into the molds, filling each about ¾ full.

    Steam the Idlis:
    Heat water in the idli steamer or pressure cooker (without the whistle/weight) and let it come to a boil. Place the idli plates in the steamer, cover, and steam the idlis for about 10-15 minutes on medium heat.

    Check for Doneness:
    To check if the idlis are done, insert a toothpick or knife into one of the idlis. If it comes out clean, the idlis are cooked. Remove the plates and let them cool for a minute.

    Remove the Idlis:
    Use a spoon or knife to gently scoop the idlis out of the molds.
