RECIPE:



Ingredients:


  • 1 cup raw peanuts (with or without skin)
  • 4 tablespoons gram flour (besan)
  • 2 tablespoons rice flour (for extra crispiness)
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon chaat masala (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 tablespoon oil (for binding)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (to make the batter)
  • Oil (for deep frying or a few teaspoons for baking)
  • Fresh curry leaves (optional, for frying)



    • Instructions:



    1. Preparing the Batter:


    Mix the Dry Ingredients:
    In a large bowl, combine gram flour (besan), rice flour, red chili powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.

    Add Peanuts:
    Add the raw peanuts to the bowl and toss them with the dry flour mixture so that each peanut is lightly coated.

    Make the Batter:
    Drizzle the oil over the peanuts and then gradually sprinkle a few tablespoons of water over the mixture. Mix well until the peanuts are coated with a thick, sticky batter. Be careful not to add too much water; you want just enough to bind the flour to the peanuts.


    2. Frying Method:


    Heat Oil:
    Heat oil in a deep pan or kadai over medium heat. Test the oil by dropping a small piece of batter; it should sizzle and rise to the surface.

    Fry the Peanuts:
    Carefully drop small batches of the batter-coated peanuts into the hot oil. You can also add a few curry leaves to the oil for extra flavor. Fry the peanuts, stirring occasionally, until they turn golden and crispy. This should take about 4-5 minutes per batch.

    Drain Excess Oil:
    Use a slotted spoon to remove the fried peanuts from the oil and place them on paper towels to drain the excess oil.


    3. Baking Method (Healthier Option):

    Preheat the Oven:
    Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and lightly grease it.

    Bake the Peanuts:
    Spread the batter-coated peanuts in a single layer on the baking sheet. Drizzle a little oil on top. Bake for 15-20 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the peanuts are golden and crispy.


    4. Final Touch:

    Sprinkle Chaat Masala:
    Once the fried or baked peanuts have cooled slightly, sprinkle chaat masala over them and toss to coat.

    Serve:
    Serve the masala peanuts warm or at room temperature as a snack.
