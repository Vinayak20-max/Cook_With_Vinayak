RECIPE:



Ingredients:


  • 200g paneer (cut into long strips or cubes)
  • 1/2 cup cornflakes (crushed)
  • 2 tbsp cornflour
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tbsp rice flour (for extra crispiness)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp chaat masala
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp black pepper powder
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Water (as needed for batter)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

    • Instructions:

    Prepare the Batter:

    In a mixing bowl, combine cornflour, all-purpose flour, rice flour, red chili powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder, garam masala, ginger-garlic paste, black pepper, and salt.
    Add water gradually to make a thick batter that can coat the paneer well.


    Crush the Cornflakes:

    Take the cornflakes and crush them coarsely using your hands or in a zip-lock bag with a rolling pin. Spread them on a plate.


    Coat the Paneer:

    Dip the paneer strips or cubes into the batter, ensuring they are well-coated.
    Roll the batter-coated paneer in the crushed cornflakes to create a crunchy layer.


    Fry the Paneer Kurkure:

    Heat oil in a deep pan or kadhai over medium heat.
    Once the oil is hot, carefully drop in the paneer pieces and fry them until golden and crispy. Do not overcrowd the pan.
    Remove the fried paneer on a kitchen towel to drain any excess oil.


    Garnish and Serve:

    Sprinkle some chaat masala over the fried paneer for an extra burst of flavor.
    Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with green chutney or ketchup.
    Optionally, serve with lemon wedges for a tangy kick.


    Enjoy your Paneer Kurkure as a crunchy appetizer or snack!
