RECIPE:



Ingredients:


For Paneer
  • 250g paneer (cottage cheese), cubed
  • 1/4 cup cornflour
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • Water (as needed)
  • Oil (for frying)
    • For Sauce
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 2 tbsp spring onion greens, chopped (for garnish)
  • 1 medium onion, diced into large chunks
  • 1 small capsicum (bell pepper), diced into large chunks
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tbsp garlic, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp ginger, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp red chili sauce
  • 1 tbsp tomato ketchup
  • 1 tsp vinegar
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper powder
  • Salt to taste

    • Instructions:



    Prepare the Paneer:
    In a bowl, mix cornflour, maida, and a pinch of salt with some water to make a smooth batter. Coat the paneer cubes in this batter.
    Shallow fry the coated paneer in hot oil until golden and crisp. Set aside on a paper towel.



    Prepare the Sauce:



    Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan. Add ginger-garlic paste and saut√© until fragrant.
    Add green chilies, sliced onions, and bell peppers. Stir-fry for a couple of minutes; keep the vegetables slightly crisp.
    Add soy sauce, red chili sauce, tomato ketchup, and vinegar. Mix well.
    Season with salt and black pepper.
    Add the cornflour slurry and cook until the sauce thickens slightly.



    Combine:

    Add the fried paneer to the sauce and toss everything together until the paneer is well-coated.
    Cook for another 2-3 minutes, letting the flavors meld.



    Garnish and Serve:

    Garnish with spring onions and serve hot!
    You can enjoy this Paneer Chilli with fried rice, noodles, or as a standalone appetizer!
