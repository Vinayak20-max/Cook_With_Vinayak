RECIPE:



Ingredients:


  • 500g boneless chicken (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 2 tbsp cornflour
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tbsp rice flour (optional for extra crispiness)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp black pepper powder
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 egg (for binding)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

    • Instructions:

    Marinate the Chicken:

    In a bowl, combine chicken pieces with ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, garam masala, black pepper powder, salt, lemon juice, and egg.
    Add cornflour, all-purpose flour, and rice flour to the marination. Mix everything well to coat the chicken evenly.
    Let the chicken marinate for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour.


    Fry the Chicken:

    Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadhai.
    Fry the marinated chicken pieces in hot oil until they turn crispy and golden brown. Fry in batches to avoid overcrowding.
    Remove the fried chicken and drain on a paper towel to remove excess oil.


    Tempering:

    In a separate pan, heat 1 tablespoon of oil.
    Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Once they crackle, add curry leaves and slit green chilies. Saut√© for a minute.
    Optionally, add a tablespoon of yogurt and a pinch of red chili powder for extra flavor. Mix and cook for a minute until the mixture thickens slightly.
    Toss the fried chicken in this tempering until the pieces are well coated.


    Garnish and Serve:

    Garnish with fried curry leaves, lemon wedges, and sprinkle a little chaat masala if desired.
    Serve hot with a side of green chutney or enjoy as a standalone snack.

    Chicken 65 is perfect as a spicy, tangy, and crispy appetizer!
