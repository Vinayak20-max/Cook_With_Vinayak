RECIPE:



Ingredients:


For Chole (Chickpea Curry):


  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (or 1 can of cooked chickpeas)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 black cardamom pod
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 2 teaspoons coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon chole masala (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)




    • For Bhature:


  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 tablespoon semolina (rava or sooji)
  • ½ cup yogurt (curd)
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (for kneading)
  • Oil (for deep frying)


    • Instructions:


    Preparing Chole (Chickpea Curry):

    Soak Chickpeas:
    If using dried chickpeas, soak them in water overnight. Drain and rinse before cooking.

    Cook Chickpeas:
    Boil the soaked chickpeas in a pressure cooker with a pinch of salt and enough water for 6-7 whistles, or until soft. If using canned chickpeas, drain and rinse.

    Make the Masala:
    Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds, cloves, bay leaf, and black cardamom. Once they crackle, add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.

    Add Ginger-Garlic Paste:
    Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.

    Add Tomatoes & Spices:
    Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and the oil separates from the mixture. Add turmeric, cumin powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.

    Add Chickpeas:
    Add the boiled chickpeas to the masala and mix well. Add some water to adjust the consistency, and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes on low heat.

    Final Touches:
    Stir in garam masala and chole masala (if using). Let the curry cook for another 5 minutes. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves

    Preparing Bhature:



    Make the Dough:
    In a large bowl, combine all-purpose flour, semolina, baking powder, baking soda, sugar, and salt. Add yogurt and mix well. Add a little water and knead into a soft dough. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 1-2 hours.

    Shape the Bhature:
    Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each ball into a circle or oval shape using a rolling pin. The thickness should be medium (not too thin or thick).

    Deep Frying:
    Heat oil in a deep pan or kadai. Once the oil is hot, carefully slide the rolled-out bhature into the oil. Press gently with a slotted spoon to help it puff up. Fry until golden brown on both sides. Drain on a paper towel to remove excess oil.
