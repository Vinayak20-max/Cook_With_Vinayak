RECIPE:



Ingredients:


  • 200 grams button mushrooms (cleaned and halved if large)
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder (optional)
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cornflour (cornstarch)
  • ½ cup breadcrumbs (or panko for extra crispiness)
  • 1 teaspoon paprika or red chili powder
  • 2-3 tablespoons milk
  • Oil (for deep frying)


    • Preparing the Mushrooms:


    Clean the Mushrooms:
    Wipe the mushrooms clean with a damp cloth or rinse them briefly under water. Pat dry completely. If the mushrooms are large, cut them in half.

    Season the Mushrooms:
    Lightly season the mushrooms with salt and pepper.

    Coating the Mushrooms:

    Prepare the Dry Mix:
    In a bowl, mix all-purpose flour, cornflour, garlic powder, black pepper powder, paprika or red chili powder, and a pinch of salt.

    Prepare the Wet Mix:
    In a separate bowl, beat the egg (or use milk for a vegetarian option).

    Prepare the Breadcrumbs:
    Spread the breadcrumbs on a plate or shallow dish.

    Coat the Mushrooms:
    First, roll the mushrooms in the dry flour mixture to coat them evenly.
    Then, dip each mushroom in the beaten egg (or milk).
    Finally, coat them with breadcrumbs, pressing gently to ensure the breadcrumbs stick well. Repeat for all mushrooms.

    Frying the Mushrooms:


    Heat the Oil:
    Heat oil in a deep frying pan or heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Test the oil by dropping a small piece of bread; it should sizzle and rise to the surface.

    Fry the Mushrooms:
    Fry the crumb-coated mushrooms in small batches for 2-3 minutes, until they turn golden brown and crispy. Avoid overcrowding the pan.

    Drain the Mushrooms:
    Use a slotted spoon to remove the mushrooms and place them on paper towels to absorb excess oil.

    Serving:
    Serve the crumb-fried mushrooms hot with lemon wedges and your favorite dipping sauce, like garlic mayo, ketchup, or spicy aioli.
