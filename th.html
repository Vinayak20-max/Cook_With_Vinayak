RECIPE:



Ingredients:


For Thandai Paste:

  • ¼ cup almonds
  • 2 tbsp cashews
  • 2 tbsp pistachios
  • 1 tbsp poppy seeds (khus khus)
  • 1 tbsp melon seeds
  • 8-10 black peppercorns
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods
  • ½ tsp fennel seeds (saunf)
  • 10-12 strands of saffron (optional)
  • 1 tbsp rose water (or 8-10 dried rose petals)
  • 2-3 tbsp sugar (adjust to taste)
  • ½ cup water (for soaking the ingredients)

    • For the Thandai Drink:

  • 4 cups chilled milk
  • 1 tsp kewra water (optional)
  • Crushed pistachios and almonds (for garnish)
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)

    • Instructions:




    1. Soak the Ingredients:

    Soak almonds, cashews, pistachios, poppy seeds, melon seeds, peppercorns, cardamom pods, fennel seeds, and saffron in ½ cup of water for 3-4 hours, or overnight.


    2. Make the Thandai Paste:

    After soaking, drain the water and transfer the soaked ingredients to a blender.
    Add sugar and rose water (or rose petals) and blend into a smooth paste. Add a little water if needed to get a creamy consistency.


    3. Prepare the Thandai Drink:

    In a large bowl or jug, pour the chilled milk.
    Stir in the Thandai paste and mix well until fully combined.
    Add kewra water (if using) and a pinch of saffron strands for extra flavor and color.


    4. Chill and Serve:

    Refrigerate the Thandai for at least 1-2 hours to allow the flavors to infuse.
    Stir well before serving.


    5. Garnish:

    Pour the chilled Thandai into glasses.
    Garnish with crushed pistachios, almonds, and a few saffron strands.



    Enjoy your rich and aromatic Thandai, perfect for a festive treat!
